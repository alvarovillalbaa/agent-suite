# CORE-EEAT Content Benchmark

> Based on [CORE-EEAT Content Benchmark](https://github.com/aaron-he-zhu/core-eeat-content-benchmark) v3.0
>
> **8 dimensions × 10 items = 80 evaluation criteria** for optimizing content visibility across AI engines (GEO) and search engines (SEO).

---

## 1. Framework Overview

### Two-System Architecture

| System | Focus | Dimensions | Items | Boundary |
|--------|-------|------------|-------|----------|
| CORE | GEO (AI Engine Optimization) | C, O, R, E | 40 | Content body |
| EEAT | SEO (Search Engine Optimization) | Exp, Ept, A, T | 40 | Author / Organization / Site |

**MECE Boundary Rule:** CORE evaluates the content body itself. EEAT evaluates the source — author, organization, and site credibility. There is no overlap.

### 8 Dimensions

| Abbr | Full Name | Core Question |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| C | Contextual Clarity | Does the content clearly answer the user's intent? |
| O | Organization | Is the content structured for both humans and machines? |
| R | Referenceability | Can AI and readers verify and cite the claims? |
| E | Exclusivity | Does the content offer something unavailable elsewhere? |
| Exp | Experience | Does the author demonstrate real-world experience? |
| Ept | Expertise | Does the author demonstrate professional expertise? |
| A | Authority | Is the author/org recognized as an authority? |
| T | Trust | Does the site meet trust and safety standards? |

### Priority Tags

- **GEO-First** 🎯 = Critical for AI engine citation
- **SEO-First** 🔍 = Critical for traditional search ranking
- **Dual** ⚡ = Important for both

## 3. Scoring System

### Per-Item Scoring

| Result | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Pass | 10 |
| Partial | 5 |
| Fail | 0 |

### Score Calculation

- **Dimension score** = sum of 10 items (0–100)
- **GEO Score** = (C + O + R + E) / 4
- **SEO Score** = (Exp + Ept + A + T) / 4
- **Total Score** = (GEO + SEO) / 2 — unweighted average for quick comparison
- **Weighted Score** = Σ (dimension_score × content_type_weight) — use for content-type-specific evaluation

### Content-Type Weight Table

| Dim | Product Review | How-to Guide | Comparison | Landing Page | Blog Post | FAQ Page | Alternative | Best-of | Testimonial |
|-----|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| C | 10% | 20% | 10% | 20% | 25% | 25% | 10% | 10% | 10% |
| O | 10% | 20% | 20% | 10% | 10% | 25% | 15% | 25% | 5% |
| R | 15% | 10% | 25% | 5% | 10% | 15% | 25% | 20% | 15% |
| E | 20% | 5% | 10% | 5% | 20% | 5% | 5% | 15% | 10% |
| Exp | 20% | 5% | 5% | 5% | 10% | 5% | 15% | 5% | 30% |
| Ept | 5% | 20% | 15% | 5% | 10% | 10% | 5% | 10% | 5% |
| A | 5% | 5% | 5% | 25% | 5% | 5% | 5% | 5% | 5% |
| T | 15% | 15% | 10% | 25% | 10% | 10% | 20% | 10% | 20% |

### Rating Scale

| Score Range | Rating |
|-------------|--------|
| 90–100 | Excellent |
| 75–89 | Good |
| 60–74 | Medium |
| 40–59 | Low |
| 0–39 | Poor |

### Veto Items

A Fail on any veto item caps the total at "Low" regardless of other scores:

- **T04** — Affiliate links without disclosure
- **C01** — Clickbait (title doesn't match content)
- **R10** — Data contradicts itself

## 6. Detailed Criteria Reference (Pass/Partial/Fail)

### C — Contextual Clarity

**C01: Intent Alignment** | Dual ⚡
- **Pass**: Title promise fully delivered; intent type clear.
- **Partial**: Mostly aligned with minor drift.
- **Fail**: Clickbait; content doesn't match title.

**C02: Direct Answer** | GEO 🎯
- **Pass**: First 150 words contain clear definition or conclusion (directly citable by AI).
- **Partial**: Answer within first 300 words with lengthy preamble.
- **Fail**: Answer buried in middle or end.

**C03: Query Coverage** | Dual ⚡
- **Pass**: Covers ≥3 query variants; appropriate entity density.
- **Partial**: 1–2 variants.
- **Fail**: Single exact query only.

**C04: Definition First** | GEO 🎯
- **Pass**: All key terms defined on first use.
- **Partial**: Most terms defined.
- **Fail**: Terms used without definition.

**C05: Topic Scope** | GEO 🎯
- **Pass**: Explicitly states "This covers X, not Y"; meets AI completeness threshold.
- **Partial**: Implied boundaries.
- **Fail**: Scope unclear; content sprawls.

**C06: Audience Targeting** | Dual ⚡
- **Pass**: Explicitly states target reader; language matches audience.
- **Partial**: Implied through difficulty.
- **Fail**: Audience unclear; inconsistent difficulty.

**C07: Semantic Coherence** | GEO 🎯
- **Pass**: Logical connectors between paragraphs; no semantic jumps.
- **Partial**: Mostly coherent with occasional jumps.
- **Fail**: Frequent logic breaks.

**C08: Use Case Mapping** | GEO 🎯
- **Pass**: Clearly states applicable/inapplicable scenarios; decision framework provided.
- **Partial**: Some scenarios mentioned.
- **Fail**: No use case guidance.

**C09: FAQ Coverage** | GEO 🎯
- **Pass**: Structured FAQ with FAQPage Schema covering long-tail follow-ups.
- **Partial**: Q&A content but not structured.
- **Fail**: No FAQ or Q&A.

**C10: Semantic Closure** | Dual ⚡
- **Pass**: Conclusion answers opening question + provides next steps.
- **Partial**: Conclusion but doesn't loop back.
- **Fail**: No conclusion or unrelated.

### O — Organization

**O01: Heading Hierarchy** | Dual ⚡ — Single H1; H2→H3 nested; no level skipping. Pass=correct hierarchy; Fail=multiple H1s or chaotic nesting.

**O02: Summary Box** | GEO 🎯 — Prominent TL;DR or Key Takeaways box. Pass=prominent; Fail=absent.

**O03: Data Tables** | GEO 🎯 — HTML tables for comparisons/specs with clear headers. Pass=structured tables; Fail=prose where tables would be better.

**O04: List Formatting** | GEO 🎯 — ~1–2 lists per 500 words; parallel items listed. Pass=appropriate density; Fail=overused or absent.

**O05: Schema Markup** | GEO 🎯 — Correct JSON-LD matching content type. Pass=correct schema; Partial=wrong type; Fail=no schema.

**O06: Section Chunking** | GEO 🎯 — Each section single topic; paragraphs 3–5 sentences. Pass=clear chunking; Fail=walls of text.

**O07: Visual Hierarchy** | SEO 🔍 — Important content bolded/highlighted. Pass=consistent emphasis; Fail=no visual hierarchy.

**O08: Anchor Navigation** | Dual ⚡ — Table of contents with anchor links. Pass=TOC+anchors+breadcrumbs; Fail=long content without navigation.

**O09: Information Density** | GEO 🎯 — High information density; no filler; consistent terminology. Pass=no filler; Fail=significant padding.

**O10: Multimedia Structure** | Dual ⚡ — Images/videos have captions; positioned purposefully. Pass=captioned+purposeful; Fail=decorative only.

### R — Referenceability

**R01: Data Precision** | GEO 🎯 — ≥5 precise data points with units; directly extractable. Pass=5+ data points; Partial=2–4; Fail=vague only.

**R02: Citation Density** | GEO 🎯 — ≥1 external citation per 500 words; ≥3 source types. Pass=meets threshold; Fail=insufficient.

**R03: Source Hierarchy** | GEO 🎯 — Primary sources prioritized; ≥3 Tier 1–2 sources. Pass=primary sources first; Fail=no authoritative sources.

**R04: Evidence-Claim Mapping** | GEO 🎯 — Every core claim immediately followed by evidence. Pass=all claims backed; Fail=multiple unbacked claims.

**R05: Methodology Transparency** | GEO 🎯 — Sample size, steps, criteria documented; reproducible. Pass=documented method; Fail=none.

**R06: Timestamp & Versioning** | Dual ⚡ — Updated <1 year; date visible; version notes. Pass=current+dated; Fail=>3 years or no date.

**R07: Entity Precision** | GEO 🎯 — Full names for entities; no vague references. Pass=all entities named; Fail=frequent vague references.

**R08: Internal Link Graph** | SEO 🔍 — Descriptive anchors forming topic clusters. Pass=descriptive anchors; Fail=no links or "click here".

**R09: HTML Semantics** | GEO 🎯 — Correct semantic tags (`<article>`, `<figure>`, `<time>`, `<cite>`). Pass=semantic tags; Fail=pure div markup.

**R10: Content Consistency** | Dual ⚡ — Data self-consistent; no broken links; corrections log. Pass=consistent+no 404s; Fail=contradictions or broken links.

### E — Exclusivity

**E01: Original Data** | GEO 🎯 — First-party data; dataset is citable. Pass=documented first-party data; Fail=all data from others.

**E02: Novel Framework** | GEO 🎯 — Named, citable original framework. Pass=named+citable; Fail=no innovation.

**E03: Primary Research** | GEO 🎯 — Documented research process. Pass=conditions+metrics+controls; Fail=none.

**E04: Contrarian View** | GEO 🎯 — Challenges consensus with data/logic. Pass=evidence-backed contrarian view; Fail=follows convention entirely.

**E05: Proprietary Visuals** | Dual ⚡ — ≥2 original infographics/charts. Pass=2+ originals; Partial=1; Fail=none.

**E06: Gap Filling** | GEO 🎯 — Covers niche questions competitors miss. Pass=clear gap filled; Fail=identical to competitors.

**E07: Practical Tools** | Dual ⚡ — ≥1 downloadable template/checklist/calculator. Pass=actionable download; Fail=none.

**E08: Depth Advantage** | GEO 🎯 — Depth clearly exceeds competitors. Pass=clearly deeper; Partial=comparable; Fail=shallower.

**E09: Synthesis Value** | GEO 🎯 — Cross-domain knowledge producing new insights. Pass=novel cross-domain synthesis; Fail=single domain only.

**E10: Forward Insights** | GEO 🎯 — Data-backed predictions with clear reasoning. Pass=predictions+evidence; Fail=past/present only.

### Exp — Experience

**Exp01** — First-person + action verb combinations. **Exp02** — ≥10 sensory words. **Exp03** — Detailed process with timeline. **Exp04** — ≥2 original images with timestamps. **Exp05** — Explicitly states testing duration. **Exp06** — ≥2 problems with solutions. **Exp07** — Clear before/after comparison. **Exp08** — Quantified experience data. **Exp09** — Multiple tests or long-term tracking. **Exp10** — Explicitly states experience limitations.

### Ept — Expertise

**Ept01** — Byline + avatar + bio >30 words. **Ept02** — Relevant professional qualifications. **Ept03** — Accurate industry jargon. **Ept04** — Technical details accurate and actionable. **Ept05** — Methodology clear and reproducible. **Ept06** — ≥2 edge cases discussed. **Ept07** — Demonstrates field's history. **Ept08** — Explicit cause-effect and tradeoffs. **Ept09** — Cross-domain knowledge integration. **Ept10** — "Reviewed by" or "Fact-checked by" labels.

### A — Authority

**A01** — Cited by authoritative sites. **A02** — "Featured in" with media logos. **A03** — Relevant industry awards. **A04** — Conference talks, publications, patents. **A05** — Brand has search volume. **A06** — Authentic reviews with real details. **A07** — Wikipedia entry or Knowledge Panel. **A08** — Brand/author consistent across web. **A09** — Partnerships with authoritative orgs. **A10** — Active and influential in professional communities.

### T — Trust

**T01** — Privacy Policy + TOS present. **T02** — Physical address or ≥2 contact methods. **T03** — Site-wide HTTPS; no warnings. **T04** — Affiliate links disclosed (**VETO if missing**). **T05** — Content standards and review process published. **T06** — Corrections page, update principles, revision history. **T07** — Ads <30% of page; no intrusive popups. **T08** — YMYL topics have disclaimers. **T09** — Reviews show authenticity signals. **T10** — Clear return policy, complaint channels, response SLA.

## 7. Calibration Examples

### C05 (Topic Scope)
- **Pass**: "This guide covers on-page SEO for WordPress WooCommerce. It does not cover technical server configuration, JavaScript SPAs, or paid search." Clear exclusions prevent scope creep.
- **Fail**: Article titled "Complete SEO Guide" covers only title tags with no mention of limited scope.

### C07 (Semantic Coherence)
- **Pass**: Database indexing article moves from "what indexes are" → "how B-tree works" → "when to add them" → "common mistakes," each section building on the previous.
- **Fail**: Article alternates between beginner explanations and advanced query optimization with no transitions.

### C08 (Use Case Mapping)
- **Pass**: "Use Redis for sub-millisecond session caching where data loss is acceptable. Use PostgreSQL for ACID-compliant transactional data. Use both together when you need fast reads with durable writes."
- **Fail**: Compares Redis and PostgreSQL features in a table but never states when to choose one over the other.

### O09 (Information Density)
- **Pass**: Every sentence introduces a fact, evidence, or advances the argument. No throat-clearing like "It is important to note that."
- **Fail**: ~30% of word count adds no new information; terminology used inconsistently.

### R03 (Source Hierarchy)
- **Pass**: Cites Google Search Central blog (Tier 1), a peer-reviewed paper (Tier 1), and Ahrefs' 11M-URL study (Tier 2).
- **Fail**: All claims reference "experts say" without naming any source.

### E04 (Contrarian View)
- **Pass**: "The common advice to always compress images to WebP ignores the 12% of global browsers lacking full WebP support (Can I Use, Jan 2025)... dual-format approach outperformed WebP-only by 3.2% in our conversion tests."
- **Fail**: No alternative viewpoints. Article treats WebP as universally beneficial.

### E09 (Synthesis Value)
- **Pass**: SaaS pricing article combines behavioral economics research (anchoring, loss aversion) + B2B sales cycle data + A/B test results to propose a novel pricing page framework.
- **Fail**: Discusses SaaS pricing purely from a feature-comparison perspective.
