# X Writing Guidelines

Based on the Matt Gray writing framework and data-driven post research.

---

## Core Principles

- Write original, specific, actionable content — avoid generic or AI-sounding copy
- Include both the WHAT and HOW — be deeply specific with numbers, timelines, and steps
- Aim for details the reader cannot find elsewhere
- Draw from proven examples and top-performing posts; never copy verbatim
- Prioritize newer, higher-quality source material over outdated references

---

## Hook Patterns

The first line determines whether the post gets read. Strong hook types:

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| Specific number claim | "I went from 0 to 50k followers doing these 7 things:" |
| Bold contrast | "Everyone says X. I did the opposite. Here's what happened:" |
| Surprising data | "95% of X posts get under 100 views. Here's the real reason:" |
| Curiosity gap | "The counterintuitive rule that changed how I write every post:" |
| Direct value | "A 5-step system for [outcome]. Save this." |
| Before/after | "One year ago I had 300 followers. Today: 80k. The difference:" |

Hook rules:
- Lead with the sharpest point, not context or background
- Be specific — generic claims get scrolled past
- No hedging ("I think", "maybe", "kind of")
- Keep it to one idea

---

## Body Rules

- Explain the HOW, not just the WHAT
- Use specific numbers, timelines, tool names, and examples
- Short paragraphs (1–3 lines) or bullets for scan-ability
- Every sentence must earn its place — cut anything that doesn't advance the point
- Avoid: "delve into", "unlock", "elevate", "game-changer", "crucial", "groundbreaking"
- Write the way a knowledgeable peer explains something, not a newsletter

---

## Thread Structure

- Use capitalized title headers — no end punctuation on headers
- Consistent spacing between sentences and bullet points
- Each tweet must stand alone but advance the narrative
- End each tweet with a banger — a mic-drop line designed for screenshots and shares
- Final tweet: always include a clear CTA

Example banger endings:
- "Most people stop before this step. That's the whole game."
- "The ones who figure this out early win by default."
- "This took me 3 years to learn. I'm handing it to you in 60 seconds."

---

## Engagement and Distribution

- Tag 4–6 relevant people or accounts per thread when genuinely applicable
- Include one clear CTA: follow, reply, bookmark, newsletter signup, or link
- Diversify examples across different contexts — don't use a single demographic or niche
- Provide concrete, usable samples: frameworks, agendas, templates, checklists
- Bookmark-worthy content outperforms pure engagement bait on long time horizons

---

## What Makes a Post Shareable

Posts get shared when they make the sharer look smart or generous. Build for that:

- Sharp insight the reader wants to claim ("I knew this")
- Useful reference they want to hand to someone ("you need to read this")
- Bold take they want to signal alignment with ("this is exactly right")
- Practical tool/framework they want to save ("this is so useful")

---

## Quality Bar

Before finalizing any post or thread:

- [ ] Hook is specific — not generic
- [ ] Body explains HOW, not just WHAT
- [ ] Numbers and examples are real and specific
- [ ] No AI filler phrases
- [ ] Final tweet has a CTA
- [ ] Reads like a person, not a template
- [ ] Each tweet in a thread can stand alone
