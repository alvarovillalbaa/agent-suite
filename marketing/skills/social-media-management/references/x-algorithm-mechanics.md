# X Algorithm Mechanics

The X algorithm measures two completely separate signals. Most people treat all engagement as equal. That's why most launches die.

---

## The Two Signals

### Signal 1 — Sourcing
**Does the algorithm show your post at all?**

Primary driver: **retweets**

No retweets = post never enters the For You feed. Ever.

Retweets are the gate. Without them, replies don't matter — nobody sees the post to begin with. This is why giveaways that require a retweet work: they're directly gaming the sourcing signal.

**What counts as a retweet for sourcing:**
- Standard retweet
- Quote tweet (even negative ones)
- Bookmarks have secondary sourcing value

### Signal 2 — Ranking
**How high does your post appear once it enters the feed?**

Primary driver: **reply chains where the original author responds back**

Each reply you post to your own post creates a new chain.
Each chain tells the algorithm this is a real, active conversation worth showing.

**The compounding mechanic:**
1. You reply to a comment → creates a chain
2. Algorithm reads: "active conversation"
3. Shows to more people
4. More people → more replies
5. More replies → higher ranking
6. Higher ranking → more visibility → repeat

This is the single highest-leverage mechanic on the platform. More chains = more ranking = more reach.

---

## The 48-Hour Reply Window

Posts are ranked and re-ranked continuously in the first 48 hours. Your reply activity within this window directly determines terminal reach.

**You will know whether a post will live or die within the first 3 hours.** Watch the retweet rate, not likes.

### The Reply Schedule

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| 0–60 min | Post goes live. Let it breathe. Organic audience only. Reply to every question thoughtfully. |
| 60–120 min | If organic velocity is strong → deploy connections and influencers. |
| 2–48 hours | Reply to every single comment. Never stop. Not for meetings, not for sleep. |
| 48+ hours | Continue monitoring. Add follow-up content if momentum continues. |

**Rule:** Launches where founders follow this exactly outperform by 2–3x.

### Why You Can't Stop

When you stop replying, the reply chain activity stops. The algorithm reads declining chain velocity as the conversation dying down and deprioritizes the post. One gap in replies can kill late-stage momentum.

**Claude Code prompt for maintaining velocity:**
```
I'm working on a launch [insert video script + product knowledge base]. 
Write 25 potential replies to people responding to my launch — keep the 
conversation going, add value, sound like a real human responding to it, 
and keep each under 280 characters, preferably less.
```

## Velocity Indicators

Track these in the first 3 hours to predict terminal reach:

| Signal | Weak | Strong | Critical |
|--------|------|--------|----------|
| Retweets (first hour) | <5 | 10–50 | 50+ |
| Reply chain depth | <3 | 5–15 | 15+ |
| Quote tweet % | <1% | 3–8% | 8%+ |
| Views/hour | <500 | 2K–10K | 10K+ |

## Influencer Deployment

**Never blast all connections at once.** Artificial spikes with no organic foundation signal inauthentic behavior and kill posts within an hour.

### The Staggering Rule

1. Wait for organic velocity to confirm (first 60 min)
2. Deploy 10–20 influencers maximum
3. Stagger posts in 30-minute windows across the deployment
4. Never deploy all influencers simultaneously

### Disclosure Requirement

All influencer posts must disclose the relationship. This is both a legal requirement (FTC rules) and a platform trust signal. Undisclosed paid posts risk account action and algorithm suppression.

---

## The Follow-Up Content Ladder

The algorithm stays "hot" on a topic for 24–48 hours after a viral post. Use this window.

| Day | Content |
|-----|---------|
| Day 1 | Main launch video / post |
| Day 2 | Meme your product — light, shareable |
| Day 3 | Deep dive on one specific feature |
| Day 4 | Tell your company story |
| Week 2 | Results, testimonials, social proof |

Each follow-up piece benefits from the topic momentum established by Day 1 and creates new reply chains that keep the original post circulating.

---

## What Kills a Launch

| Mistake | Why it fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Blasting all connections at once | Algorithm reads as artificial spike → suppresses post |
| Going quiet after posting | Reply chain velocity drops → ranking collapses |
| Launching with no organic base | Nothing to amplify → influencers wasted |
| Deploying influencers before organic velocity | No foundation → post flagged as inauthentic |
| Single CTA with no giveaway mechanic | No retweet driver → never enters For You feed |
| Stopping replies after 8 hours | Chains die → ranking drops on a post that could have kept growing |
