# Survey to Lead Magnet

Use this reference when the user has survey responses and wants to turn them into segmented lead magnets, content offers, or pain-point-driven acquisition assets.

This is not generic content ideation. It is a conversion workflow: cluster real pain language, rank the segments, and generate the best lead magnet concept for each one.

---

## When to Use

- the user has Typeform, Google Forms, SurveyMonkey, or CSV survey exports
- the team wants to identify the most commercial pain-point segments
- the user wants lead magnet ideas grounded in actual respondent language
- content strategy needs stronger demand signals than brainstorming alone

---

## Inputs to Collect

Minimum:
- survey export or pasted responses
- which columns contain pain points, frustrations, desired outcomes, or JTBD-style answers
- target audience or ICP, if known

Helpful:
- offer type already in market
- primary acquisition channel
- what happens after the lead magnet converts

---

## Workflow

### 1. Normalize the responses

- focus on free-text pain, challenge, or goal fields
- remove obvious noise, duplicates, and empty answers
- preserve verbatim phrases that reveal buyer language

### 2. Cluster by pain point

Group responses into a small number of meaningful themes:
- recurring problem
- recurring desired outcome
- recurring blocker or objection

Name the cluster using the buyer's language when possible, not internal taxonomy.

### 3. Rank the segments

Rank each segment using:
- **size**: how many respondents express this pain
- **commercial potential**: how closely the pain maps to buying intent, urgency, and product fit
- **message clarity**: how clearly the pain can be articulated in copy

Prefer the segment that is both painful and monetizable over the segment that is merely common.

### 4. Pick the right lead magnet format

Recommended mapping:

- **Guide** for complex education or strategic problems
- **Checklist** for process and execution problems
- **Template** for repeated tasks or fill-in-the-blank work
- **Calculator** for quantified tradeoffs or ROI framing
- **Swipe file** for examples, inspiration, or messaging teardown

Choose the format that gets the user to an immediate "this is useful" moment.

### 5. Generate the brief

For each high-priority segment, produce:
- title
- format
- hook
- promise
- 5-7 section outline
- CTA
- recommended distribution channel

---

## Deliverable Format

### 1. Segment Table

For each segment:
- **Segment name**
- **Representative quotes**
- **Respondent count**
- **Commercial potential**
- **Why this segment matters**

### 2. Lead Magnet Briefs

For each recommended brief:
- **Title**
- **Format**
- **Audience**
- **Core hook**
- **Promise**
- **Outline**
- **CTA**
- **Best channel**

### 3. Prioritization

Rank the top opportunities:
1. highest-conviction lead magnet to build now
2. next-best segment to test
3. lower-priority or lower-fit ideas to defer

---

## Quality Bar

- segment names reflect buyer language, not internal categorization
- quotes are verbatim and representative
- the chosen format matches the pain, not content-team preference
- the CTA leads to a real next step in the funnel
- the brief is specific enough to create without another strategy round

---

## Failure Modes

- clustering by superficial keywords instead of underlying pain
- choosing the largest segment even when it has weak buying intent
- producing generic ebook ideas that could fit any audience
- losing the original survey language during summarization
- proposing a lead magnet with no clear distribution path or next CTA
