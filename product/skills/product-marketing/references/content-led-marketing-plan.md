# Content-Led Marketing Plan

Use this reference when the user needs a practical marketing plan centered on audience building, content, email capture, and consistency before paid acquisition.

This is intentionally biased toward organic traction first. Use it when the user needs a minimalist plan that starts with time, insight, and consistency rather than an ad budget.

---

## When to Use

- the user wants a marketing plan for a product with some traction but limited marketing system
- the team needs a primary content platform, posting cadence, and email-list strategy
- the user asks how to build an audience, "build in public," or market without ads
- the team wants a sustainable content engine before paid acquisition

---

## Preconditions and Readiness Check

Confirm as much of this as possible before writing a plan:

- there is a real product, offer, or service to point people toward
- the team understands the target customer well enough to create useful content
- there is some proof of demand, paying users, or repeat usage
- founders or operators can speak credibly from direct experience

If these are weak, say so. A content plan cannot compensate for missing product clarity or nonexistent proof.

---

## Core Principle

Marketing is sales at scale. The job is to create enough relevance and trust that more people move through the funnel without one-to-one persuasion every time.

Start by spending time, not money:
- publish useful ideas
- document what you are learning
- build an owned audience via email
- use paid spend only after the audience, message, and customer profile are better understood

---

## Funnel to Plan Against

Structure the plan around the full customer journey:

1. `Engage` — someone encounters the content
2. `Follow` — they choose to keep hearing from you
3. `Research` — they click through to learn more
4. `Consider` — they evaluate fit, price, and proof
5. `Buy` — they convert

Do not jump from content ideation straight to purchase intent. Each content asset should move people to the next stage.

## Community, Audience, and Channel Selection

### Community vs audience

- `Community` is the group you genuinely belong to and can learn from
- `Audience` is the broader set of people you can reach with a point of view, teachable insight, or useful content

The strongest content plans usually start in community and then expand into audience.

### Channel selection rules

- Pick one primary platform first
- Add a second only if the team can maintain consistency
- Avoid thin presence across many platforms
- Match the channel to how the audience already learns and shares

Examples:
- LinkedIn for B2B operator and buyer education
- X/Twitter for public learning, short-form insights, and founder-led commentary
- YouTube for tutorials, product education, and reusable long-form content
- Email for the owned audience layer across any primary channel

---

## The Three Content Levels

Build the plan across three content modes:

### 1. Educate

Teach what the team knows:
- explain recurring customer problems
- share frameworks, checklists, and decision criteria
- answer questions prospects ask before buying

This is usually the best starting point for early-stage consistency.

### 2. Inspire

Make progress and struggle visible:
- show the journey
- explain decisions, mistakes, and tradeoffs
- tell the story behind the product, customer wins, or operator lessons

This builds trust and affinity, not just utility.

### 3. Entertain

Package insight in a form people want to share:
- pattern breaks
- strong hooks
- light contrarian takes
- memorable framing

This has the broadest reach, but it is hardest to do well. It should not replace substance.

## Email: Build the Owned Audience

Treat email as the durable layer of the marketing plan.

### Why

- social platforms are rented distribution
- email is a direct channel with less algorithm risk
- subscriber growth compounds over time

### What to offer

Use one lead capture offer aligned with the core audience pain:
- guide
- checklist
- template
- resource roundup
- newsletter promise with a clear value proposition

If the user needs deeper offer design, route to `lead-magnet-strategy.md`.

### Collection points

- primary site CTA
- content upgrades in high-intent articles
- founder or company profile CTA
- webinar or event registration
- thank-you-page cross-promotion between assets

## Build-in-Public Guidance

Good "build in public" content is specific and useful, not self-referential noise.

Share:
- what changed
- what you learned
- what surprised you
- what you tested
- what failed and why

Avoid:
- empty status updates
- performative vulnerability with no audience value
- constant self-promotion without teaching or insight

---

## Content Calendar Rules

Keep the plan sustainable:

- choose 1-2 channels max plus email
- define a realistic cadence the team can maintain for 8-12 weeks
- bias toward repeatable formats, not bespoke campaigns
- reuse material across formats where it makes sense

Example cadence:
- primary social platform: 3 posts per week
- long-form asset: 1 per week or every other week
- newsletter: weekly or bi-weekly

Consistency matters more than bursty output.

---

## When to Spend Money

Paid acquisition is not step one.

Consider paid only when:
- the customer profile is clear
- organic content already reveals resonant topics or hooks
- there is proof that the offer converts
- downstream economics are understood

Before acquisition spend, prefer spending on:
- better content
- stronger lead magnets
- better landing pages
- retention, loyalty, or referral moments for existing customers

---

## Deliverable Contract

When creating a content-led marketing plan, deliver:

### 1. Strategic setup

- audience
- positioning angle for content
- readiness caveats or prerequisites

### 2. Channel plan

- primary platform
- optional secondary platform
- why each channel fits
- publishing cadence

### 3. Content matrix

For each level (`Educate`, `Inspire`, `Entertain`):
- 3-5 concrete content ideas
- best format
- target funnel stage

### 4. Email plan

- list-building offer
- capture points
- newsletter or follow-up concept

### 5. Build-in-public plan

- what to share consistently
- what to avoid
- which team member voice should lead

### 6. Spend guidance

- whether paid should be deferred, tested, or scaled
- what signals should trigger that decision

---

## Failure Modes

- giving the user a generic "post more on social" plan
- spreading effort across too many channels
- producing content ideas disconnected from customer questions or product insight
- treating email as an afterthought
- recommending paid before the message and audience are working organically
- confusing authenticity with low-quality, low-signal posting
