# Lead Magnet Strategy

Use this reference when the user wants to create, plan, evaluate, or improve a lead magnet for email capture or lead generation.

This is the general strategy layer. If the request is specifically about turning survey responses into ranked pain-point segments and then choosing a lead magnet, load `survey-to-lead-magnet.md` directly.

---

## When to Use

- the user asks what to offer in exchange for an email
- the team wants a checklist, template, guide, quiz, assessment, webinar, or resource library
- the user needs gating, form-field, thank-you-page, or delivery guidance for a content offer
- the lead magnet exists but is underperforming and needs a stronger positioning or distribution plan
- the user needs a lead magnet that naturally leads into trial, demo, or product adoption

---

## Context to Gather

Minimum:
- what the product does
- who the ideal customer is
- the main problem the product solves
- the primary goal: list growth, lead quality, product education, pipeline, or activation

Helpful:
- current lead capture channels and conversion rate
- existing offers, if any
- existing content or internal assets that could be repurposed
- buyer stage: awareness, consideration, or decision
- constraints: time, design bandwidth, promotion budget

If `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` exists, read it first and only ask for gaps.

## Lead Magnet Principles

### 1. Solve one specific problem

- Pick one painful job, blocker, or decision.
- Narrow beats broad. "Migration checklist for teams switching from spreadsheets" is stronger than "Operations guide."

### 2. Match the buyer stage

| Stage | Goal | Best-fit formats |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Teach the problem | Checklist, cheat sheet, beginner guide, quiz |
| Consideration | Help compare or evaluate | Assessment, comparison template, case-study bundle, webinar |
| Decision | Help implement or justify | Template, ROI calculator, implementation guide, trial companion |

### 3. High perceived value, low time-to-value

- It should look paid, but feel fast to use.
- Prefer assets that create an immediate "I can use this today" moment.
- If the offer takes too long to consume, conversion and downstream usage both drop.

### 4. Natural path to product

- The lead magnet should solve a problem adjacent to the product, not unrelated top-of-funnel trivia.
- It should create awareness of the gap your product fills or showcase your expertise in the category.

### 5. Easy to consume

- Use one primary format, not a bundle of mismatched deliverables.
- Keep access simple and mobile-friendly.
- Avoid tools or file formats the audience is unlikely to use.

## Format Selection Guide

| Format | Best for | Effort | Time to create |
|---|---|---|---|
| Checklist | Operational tasks, audits, process steps | Low | 1-2 hours |
| Cheat sheet | Reference material, shortcuts, summaries | Low | 2-4 hours |
| Template | Repeatable workflows, planning, fill-in-the-blank work | Low-Medium | 2-8 hours |
| Swipe file | Examples, inspiration, copy teardown | Medium | 4-8 hours |
| Guide / ebook | Deeper education and category authority | High | 1-3 weeks |
| Email mini-course | Education plus nurture sequencing | Medium | 1-2 weeks |
| Quiz / assessment | Segmentation, self-diagnosis, engagement | Medium | 1-2 weeks |
| Webinar | Authority, objections, live Q&A | Medium | ~1 week prep |
| Resource library | Ongoing value and repeat visits | High | Ongoing |

Use the lowest-effort format that still creates a strong value exchange.

## Gating Strategy

### Gating options

| Approach | When to use | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|
| Full gate | High-value or bottom-funnel offers | More capture, less reach |
| Partial gate | Strong asset that benefits from preview | Balance of reach and conversion |
| Ungated + optional capture | Top-of-funnel education, brand building | More reach, lower capture |
| Content upgrade | Offer tightly matched to one content piece | High intent, narrower scale |

### Form-field guidance

- `Email only` for top-of-funnel and broad acquisition
- `Email + name` when personalization matters
- `Email + company/role` when qualification matters enough to justify lower conversion
- Multi-field forms only for genuinely high-value offers such as webinars, demos, or assessments

Ask for the minimum needed. Every extra field adds friction.

### Value-exchange framing

- make the outcome explicit
- show a preview, sample, or mockup
- reduce risk with a clear privacy reassurance
- add proof when available: download count, customer logos, or testimonial

## Delivery and Conversion Path

### Landing page structure

1. Benefit-driven headline
2. Visual preview or mockup
3. "What's inside" bullets
4. Social proof
5. Minimal form with clear CTA
6. FAQ for friction or trust concerns

### Delivery options

| Method | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| Instant download | Immediate gratification | Weaker email verification |
| Email delivery | Verifies email, starts relationship | Small delay |
| Thank-you page + email | Best balance of access and follow-up | Slightly more setup |
| Drip delivery | Good for courses or sequences | Slower time-to-value |

### Thank-you page rules

Do not waste the post-conversion moment. Include:
- confirmation of delivery
- the next step: demo, trial, consultation, product walkthrough, or related content
- optional share action
- one relevant follow-on resource

---

## Distribution Plan

Prioritize channels that match audience intent:

- blog CTAs and content upgrades
- homepage, pricing, or feature-page placements
- exit-intent or scroll-triggered popups
- social posts and profile CTAs
- retargeting or paid amplification for proven offers
- partner newsletters, webinars, or co-promotion

Content upgrades usually outperform generic sidebar offers because intent is already contextual.

## Measurement Plan

### Core metrics

| Metric | What it answers |
|---|---|
| Landing-page conversion rate | Is the offer compelling enough to convert? |
| Cost per lead | Is acquisition efficient enough for the channel? |
| Lead-to-trial or lead-to-demo rate | Are we attracting qualified leads? |
| Lead-to-customer rate | Does the offer bring in real buying intent? |
| Email engagement | Does the asset and follow-up sequence feel relevant? |
| Unsubscribe rate after delivery | Did we create the wrong expectation? |
| Time to downstream conversion | How quickly does this offer produce value? |

### Benchmarks to reason from

- warm-traffic capture pages often convert materially better than cold traffic
- content upgrades usually outperform generic sitewide offers
- lead quality matters more than raw opt-in volume for bottom-funnel offers

### Good A/B test candidates

- headline angle
- format on the same pain point
- full gate vs partial preview
- form fields
- CTA copy
- delivery method

If test design becomes the main task, hand off to `../product-development/references/experiment-playbook.md` and `../product-development/references/statistics-reference.md`.

## Deliverable Contract

When producing a lead magnet strategy, deliver:

### 1. Recommendation

- format
- topic
- target buyer stage
- why this format fits the audience and goal
- estimated effort and timeline

### 2. Content brief

- core promise
- outline or asset components
- what makes the offer useful immediately
- what should be shown in the preview/mockup

### 3. Capture and delivery plan

- gating approach
- form fields
- landing-page structure
- delivery method
- thank-you-page next step

### 4. Distribution plan

- primary channels
- contextual placements
- optional paid amplification

### 5. Measurement plan

- KPIs
- lead-quality checks
- first A/B test to run

---

## Failure Modes

- generic ebook ideas that could fit any audience
- choosing a format because it sounds premium, not because it matches the pain
- gating top-of-funnel education too aggressively
- asking for too much form information too early
- producing an offer with no clear path into product adoption
- optimizing for downloads while ignoring lead quality
