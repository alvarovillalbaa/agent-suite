# ICP Research

Use this lane when the user needs to define, sharpen, or operationalize an
ideal customer profile.

## Objective

Turn a broad market into a targetable profile that GTM teams can act on.

The result should answer:

- who to target
- who to avoid
- which segments deserve priority
- which titles and buying roles matter
- what filters should be used for account selection and prospecting

## Scope

Clarify:

- what product or offer the ICP is for
- whether the work is for marketing, outbound, partnerships, or general strategy
- geography and company-size boundaries
- whether the user needs a strategic ICP, a prospecting filter set, or both

If the user wants list-building, target-account ranking, or lead qualification
after segment definition, also load
`references/prospect-enrichment-and-qualification.md`.

If Exa MCP tools are available and the user wants public-web company discovery,
people discovery, or list building at scale, also load
`references/exa-advanced-search-categories.md`.

## Inputs To Seek

Best inputs, in priority order:

1. won customers or strongest users
2. retained or expanding accounts
3. case studies and customer proof
4. loss reasons and bad-fit accounts
5. sales notes, CRM patterns, or product usage clues
6. competitor positioning if internal data is thin

If internal evidence is missing, say the ICP is provisional.

## ICP Dimensions

Default to these dimensions:

- industry or vertical
- employee count
- revenue band if relevant
- geography
- business model
- maturity or funding stage
- tool stack or workflow environment
- pain intensity
- urgency or trigger events
- buying committee shape

Also define disqualifiers:

- segments that churn or fail to activate
- titles that sound senior but do not own the budget or problem
- company sizes that create weak ACV or long unproductive cycles
- adjacent segments that look similar but buy for different reasons

## People Layer

For each strong-fit segment, define:

- champion title
- economic buyer
- technical evaluator
- likely blockers

If the user asks for lead generation or prospecting, transform this into
structured filters, then enrich or qualify only the best matches.

For search-driven prospecting, also decompose Tier 1 and Tier 2 segments into
6-12 mutually distinct micro-verticals or query families before scaling the
search. Each micro-vertical should have:

- a company pattern
- a trigger or momentum signal
- exclusions
- follow-on people filters if needed

## Scoring Model

Use a simple segment score across:

1. pain intensity
2. ability to buy
3. urgency or trigger density
4. fit with product strengths
5. expected GTM efficiency

Use labels:

- `Tier 1`
- `Tier 2`
- `Tier 3`

Do not create more than three tiers unless the user explicitly needs them.

## Mandatory Outputs

### 1. ICP statement

Write one clean statement:

> We should prioritize [segment] with [key traits] because [reason].

### 2. Segment table

Use these columns:

| Segment | Fit | Why it fits | Why it may fail | Priority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

### 3. Account filters

Translate the ICP into:

- industry tags
- employee ranges
- geography filters
- stage or funding filters
- technographic filters
- exclusion filters

### 4. Title map

Use these rows:

- primary champion
- economic buyer
- evaluator
- likely non-buyer title to avoid

### 5. Trigger events

List the buying signals that make the segment more attractive:

- hiring
- funding
- leadership change
- compliance pressure
- stack change
- product launch
- market expansion

### 6. Prospecting next step

If the user wants list-building, end with:

- the exact company filters
- the exact people filters
- 6-12 micro-vertical or query-family expansions if search coverage matters
- what to enrich next
- what to avoid enriching yet

## Judgment Rules

- do not confuse TAM with ICP
- do not target everyone who could buy; target who is most likely to buy now
- bad-fit segments are as important as good-fit segments
- titles without real buying power should not be treated as top targets
- if the evidence is weak, say the ICP is a hypothesis to validate

## Recommended Close

End with:

1. Tier 1 segment
2. who to exclude
3. best trigger to hunt for
4. exact next move for sales or marketing
