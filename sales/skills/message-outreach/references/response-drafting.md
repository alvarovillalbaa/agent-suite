# Response Drafting

Use this reference when the user is replying to a prospect, handling an objection, or drafting a sensitive message.

## First classify the response

- question
- objection
- follow-up after silence
- delay or no-fit response
- pricing or policy boundary
- escalation or frustrated stakeholder

## Context to pull in

- previous thread content
- prior commitments or dates
- account context
- stakeholder level
- known risks or approvals required

## Default response structure

1. Acknowledge the situation or question
2. Give the direct answer or update
3. Clarify what happens next
4. Close with a clear ownership signal

## Tone selection

- **Professional:** routine updates or early-stage conversations
- **Direct:** active deal motion, scheduling, pricing, or objection handling
- **Empathetic:** delay, confusion, or mild frustration
- **Executive:** senior stakeholder, short and outcome-first
- **Technical:** practitioner questions that need specifics

## Quality checks

- No commitments beyond what the sender can defend
- No vague time language when a concrete date is possible
- No roadmap leakage or invented certainty
- No evasion of the actual question
- No unnecessary length for the channel

## Internal notes worth returning

For delicate replies, include brief internal notes on:

- why this tone was chosen
- what to verify before sending
- risks or escalation points
- suggested follow-up timing
