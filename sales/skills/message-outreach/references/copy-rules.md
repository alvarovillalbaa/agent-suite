# Copy Rules

Use this reference when drafting or reviewing cold outbound email, follow-up sequences, or any message where delivery rate and reply rate both matter.

---

## First sentence rules

**Never start with:**
- "I" — e.g., "I came across your company..."
- "We" — e.g., "We help companies like yours..."
- "Our team" — e.g., "Our team specializes in..."
- "I wanted to" — e.g., "I wanted to reach out because..."
- "Hope this finds you well" or any version of it
- "My name is..." — never in Step 1; save for later steps only if necessary

**Always start with one of:**
- Prospect's company name: "{{companyName}}'s recent expansion into..."
- A specific market observation: "Most [industry] teams we talk to are dealing with..."
- A specific finding: "Your [blog post / job listing / product launch] on..."
- A relevant trend: "Since [relevant event] in [industry]..."

The first sentence earns the second. If it does not make the prospect think "this is relevant to me," the email is finished before the body starts.

---

## Body length by step

| Step | Max sentences | Purpose |
|------|--------------|---------|
| Step 1 | 3 | Open + value + CTA — nothing else |
| Steps 2–4 | 3–5 | New angle or asset — not a repeat of the prior touch |
| Step 5 (bump) | 1–2 | "Still relevant?" style |
| Step 6 (breakup) | 2–3 | Leave value, leave the door open |

Cut ruthlessly. If a step is longer than the limit, remove sentences until it fits. Longer is not more persuasive.

---

## CTA rules

**Soft asks (preferred for Steps 1–3):**
- "Worth a look?"
- "Want the data?"
- "Does this match what you're seeing?"
- "Relevant to what you're working on?"
- "Happy to share what we found — useful?"

**Hard asks (use only in Step 4+ after engagement signals):**
- Avoid in early steps: "Book a call with me", "Schedule 30 minutes", "Let's hop on a call", "Are you free Thursday?"
- These presume interest before it has been established

One CTA per touch. Do not offer options — make the ask singular and clear.

---

## Link rules

| Step | Links allowed |
|------|--------------|
| Step 1 | None (deliverability and trust) |
| Steps 2–3 | Max 1 link, only if genuinely useful: a case study, report, or tool |
| Breakup email | 1 real link to useful content — not a sales page or form |
| Any step | Never link to a landing page with a form in Steps 1–2 — signals spray-and-pray |

Never hallucinate URLs. All links must be verified real pages before use.

---

## Subject lines

- Length: 3–7 words (sweet spot)
- No exclamation points
- No all-caps
- No emoji in B2B cold email unless the target persona clearly expects it
- Best patterns:
  - Question: "Quick question, {{firstName}}"
  - Observation: "{{companyName}}'s pipeline strategy"
  - Specificity: "Saw your post on [topic]"
  - Intrigue: "One thing we noticed"
- A/B test 2 subject line variants on Step 1 only — wait for 100+ sends per variant before calling a winner
- Do not A/B test body copy in early campaigns; isolate the subject line variable first

---

## Social proof and stats framing

**Preferred (observation framing):**
> "Most brands we audit are leaving 30–40% of their SEO traffic unconverted."

**Avoid (study/claim framing):**
> "According to our data, 73% of brands have this problem."

Observation sounds like earned experience. Study framing sounds like a marketing claim. Prospects believe the former.

**Never fabricate:**
- Specific client names unless verified and approved
- Revenue numbers or percentage improvements unless you have the actual data
- Case study specifics — if you cannot verify it, generalize it
- URLs — all links must exist and be reachable

---

## Deliverability signals to avoid

In any outbound email:

- Spam words in subject or body: "free", "guarantee", "no risk", "limited time", "act now", "click here"
- More than 1 link (ideally 0 in Steps 1–2)
- Images of any kind in cold email
- HTML formatting — plain text only
- All-caps words
- Exclamation points in subject lines
- Tracking links on root domain instead of a subdomain

---

## Tone calibration

- Peer-to-peer, not vendor-to-prospect
- Curious, not desperate
- Specific, not generic
- Short, not comprehensive
- Human, not corporate

If the draft sounds like a marketing email, rewrite it. Cold email that converts reads like a text from a knowledgeable peer who noticed something specific.

---

## Expert panel check (for high-stakes sequences)

Before launching a sequence that represents significant effort or a named account, run a simulated panel review:

1. Score these dimensions each 0–100: subject line curiosity, first-sentence pattern interrupt, body clarity and brevity, CTA softness and specificity, sequence flow and escalation logic, deliverability risk signals, personalization believability
2. Identify the top 3 weaknesses per round
3. Revise copy addressing each weakness
4. Repeat until aggregate score reaches 90+
5. Show the iteration trail — rounds of scoring are part of the quality record

Panel perspectives to simulate:
- **Reply rate lens** — Does this email get a "yes" or "tell me more"? Is the offer clear and brief?
- **Frame and status lens** — Does the sender appear credible and high-status? Is there scarcity or proof?
- **Respect lens** — Is this genuinely useful to the recipient, or just noise?
- **Pattern interrupt lens** — Does Step 1 stop the scroll? Does the subject + first sentence earn the next sentence?
- **Research depth lens** — Does the email prove the sender knows this specific prospect?
- **Sequence logic lens** — Does each follow-up add new value rather than just bumping?
- **Deliverability lens** — Are there spam triggers? Is the structure plain-text safe?
