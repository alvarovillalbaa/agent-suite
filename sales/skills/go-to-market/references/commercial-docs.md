# Commercial Documents

Load this file when the task involves proposals, statements of work, NDAs, MSAs, or implementation-backed close support.

## Principle

These documents are sales-enablement artifacts with legal and delivery consequences. Use them to clarify scope, reduce ambiguity, and help a deal close. Do not imply they replace legal counsel.

## Common artifact choices

- Proposal: use when the buyer needs the story, scope, pricing, and plan in one document
- Statement of Work: use when the commercial agreement exists and execution scope must be pinned down
- NDA: use when confidential exchange needs formal protection
- MSA: use when a reusable commercial framework is needed across multiple projects or orders

## Information to gather first

Before drafting, confirm:

- document type
- parties and legal names
- jurisdiction if it matters
- engagement model: fixed price, hourly, retainer, pilot, or subscription
- scope summary
- value or pricing structure
- timeline or term
- unusual requirements such as IP assignment, subcontractors, or data-processing terms

## Proposal structure

A strong proposal usually includes:

- client context and problem
- proposed approach
- scope and deliverables
- timeline and milestones
- assumptions and exclusions
- pricing and commercial terms
- next steps

## SOW structure

Keep SOWs concrete:

- deliverables
- milestones
- acceptance criteria
- dependencies
- change-control rules
- roles and responsibilities

## Legal caution points

Flag these explicitly when they matter:

- payment terms
- IP ownership or assignment
- liability cap
- termination rights
- confidentiality duration
- dispute resolution
- data-processing and privacy obligations

For high-value or jurisdiction-sensitive work, recommend attorney review rather than acting certain.
