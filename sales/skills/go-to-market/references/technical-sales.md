# Technical Sales

Load this file when the task involves technical discovery, RFP/RFI support, demo design, proof-of-concept planning, or technical proposal support for a deal.

## Operating model

Use a five-phase motion:

1. Discovery and research
2. Solution design
3. Demo and stakeholder validation
4. POC and evaluation
5. Proposal and closing support

Do not skip straight from "interesting prospect" to "proposal" if the deal depends on technical proof.

## 1. Discovery and research

Capture:

- business driver and buying trigger
- current workflow or architecture
- integration requirements and constraints
- security, compliance, or data-handling requirements
- must-have vs nice-to-have requirements
- incumbent or competitor context

Minimum gate before progressing:

- the team can articulate the must-have requirements
- obvious red-flag gaps are visible
- the opportunity is still worth pursuing

Good output:

- requirements map
- technical discovery brief
- initial risk list

## 2. Solution design

Map product capabilities to the requirement set.

Decide:

- what is covered out of the box
- what needs configuration or services work
- what requires roadmap, workaround, or custom build
- where the product is genuinely differentiated
- where competitors are stronger

Do not claim differentiation unless it maps to a customer priority.

Good output:

- solution narrative
- gap register
- competitive comparison tied to the buyer's priorities

## 3. Demo and stakeholder validation

Build the demo around the must-have requirements and buying context, not a generic product tour.

Include:

- role-specific talking points
- proof for each critical requirement
- clear handling for known objections
- a feedback-capture plan after the demo

If a must-have requirement is not shown or credibly answered, treat the demo as incomplete.

## 4. POC and evaluation

Run a POC only when it answers a real buying question.

Define before the POC starts:

- scope
- environment and owners
- timeline
- success criteria
- evaluation dimensions
- go/no-go rule

Suggested evaluation dimensions:

- functionality
- performance
- integration
- usability
- supportability

If success criteria are undefined, the POC will drift into unpaid consulting.

## 5. Proposal and closing support

Convert proof into a close path.

Include:

- implementation plan
- timeline and dependencies
- assumptions and exclusions
- evidence from demos, security review, or POC
- remaining objections and responses
- commercial handoff notes for pricing and packaging discussions

Useful artifacts:

- technical proposal
- implementation outline
- risk register
- success criteria recap

## Decision rules

- Treat missing technical proof as a GTM risk, not just a sales nuisance.
- Escalate when the deal requires roadmap commitments the product cannot honor.
- Prefer a narrow, defensible proof path over a broad, weak one.
- Use competitive analysis to sharpen the win strategy, not to produce a vanity matrix.
