# Revenue Operations

Load this file when the task involves pipeline inspection, forecasting, board-style revenue reporting, GTM efficiency, or unit-economics review.

## Core scorecard

At minimum, evaluate:

- pipeline coverage ratio
- stage conversion rates
- sales velocity
- deal aging
- concentration risk
- forecast confidence

For SaaS-style efficiency reviews, also evaluate:

- Magic Number
- LTV:CAC
- CAC payback
- burn multiple
- Rule of 40
- net dollar retention

## Core formulas and thresholds

Use these as default heuristics unless the user provides company-specific targets.

- Pipeline coverage ratio = total qualified pipeline / quota target
- Healthy default coverage range: roughly `3x-4x`
- Sales velocity = `(opportunities * average deal size * win rate) / average sales cycle`
- Concentration risk becomes material when a single deal is more than about `40%` of the pipeline
- Magic Number target: `>0.75`
- LTV:CAC target: `>3:1`
- CAC payback target: `<18 months`
- Burn multiple target: `<2x`
- Rule of 40 target: `>40%`
- Net dollar retention target: `>110%`

Treat these as directional operating thresholds, not laws of nature.

## Pipeline review workflow

1. Check coverage against quota or target.
2. Inspect funnel shape and conversion rates by stage.
3. Flag deals aging beyond a sensible threshold for their stage.
4. Flag concentration risk if too much of the forecast depends on one or two deals.
5. Produce concrete actions, not just diagnosis.

Typical action buckets:

- create more coverage
- unblock stage progression
- recover or close out stale deals
- reduce dependence on one large opportunity
- tighten qualification

## Forecast review workflow

Evaluate forecast quality through:

- conversion reality
- deal age and slippage
- rep confidence vs evidence
- concentration risk
- historical forecast bias if available

Do not present a precise forecast when the underlying evidence is weak.

## GTM efficiency interpretation

Use efficiency metrics to decide whether growth is durable.

Questions to answer:

- Is the business turning spend into new ARR efficiently?
- Is payback fast enough for the company's cash position?
- Is retention strong enough to justify the current acquisition model?
- Are we scaling with healthy economics or just buying noisy growth?

## Output expectation

A strong revops readout should include:

- current state
- the few metrics that matter most
- what is broken
- what to do next
- what would change the conclusion
