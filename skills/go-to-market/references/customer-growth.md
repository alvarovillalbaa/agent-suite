# Customer Growth

Load this file when the task involves health scoring, churn risk, renewals, account prioritization, net retention, or expansion planning.

## Core posture

Treat customer growth as part of GTM, not a separate afterthought. The same motion that wins customers should inform how they are kept and expanded.

Organize account analysis around four dimensions:

- usage and product adoption
- engagement and sentiment
- support load and unresolved friction
- relationship strength and commercial posture

## Health assessment

Look for evidence in these areas:

- login frequency or sustained usage
- breadth and depth of feature adoption
- DAU/MAU or recurring engagement patterns when relevant
- meeting attendance, survey signals, or sponsor engagement
- ticket volume, escalations, and resolution speed
- executive sponsorship and multi-threading
- renewal sentiment and commercial openness

The point is not to produce a fake-precise score. The point is to separate healthy, neutral, and at-risk accounts using observable signals.

## Churn-risk diagnosis

Bias toward these signal groups:

- usage decline
- engagement drop
- support or product-friction signals
- relationship deterioration
- commercial factors such as renewal timing, pricing pressure, or procurement friction

For each at-risk account, answer:

- what changed
- how severe the risk is
- what intervention could still change the outcome
- who should own the intervention

## Expansion analysis

Look for three expansion patterns:

- upsell: more of the current product or higher tier
- cross-sell: adjacent modules or capabilities
- expansion: more seats, teams, or departments

Good expansion indicators include:

- high seat utilization
- strong module adoption with obvious whitespace
- healthy product usage plus supportive stakeholder signals
- demand from adjacent teams or functions

Do not push expansion on unhealthy accounts. Fix value realization first.

## Customer portfolio review

If the task covers many accounts, split them into:

- save now
- stabilize
- expand

For each bucket, recommend the smallest set of actions that would matter.

## Metrics that matter

Depending on the task, use:

- gross retention
- net revenue retention
- logo churn
- revenue churn
- expansion rate
- renewal rate

If acquisition looks strong but these metrics are weak, call out the GTM issue directly.
