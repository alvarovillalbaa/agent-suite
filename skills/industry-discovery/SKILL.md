# Industry Discovery Skill

Uncover the latest discoveries, breakthroughs, or pressing problems in a target discipline or industry (e.g. HR, hiring, engagement, performance, change management) over a recent time window.

## Modes

1. **Discoveries** – breakthroughs, studies, or technologies (e.g. AI in recruitment, engagement analytics).
2. **Problems** – specific challenges reported by leaders (e.g. HR Directors, C-suite) in the last 7 days or specified window.

## Scope

- Identify **3–5** findings; exclude general business trends or out-of-date items.
- Prioritize findings relevant to the user’s role and context (e.g. HR Directors, Talent Ops).
- Default window: **last 7 days** unless the user specifies otherwise.

## Evidence

- Source from reputable outlets (e.g. SHRM, Gartner, Forbes, academic journals) or recent social posts with **inline citations**.
- Mark speculation as **(opinion)**.
- Include quantitative data (e.g. “X% efficiency gain”) or qualitative insights from named leaders.
- If data is limited, note the gap and ask for clarification.

## Depth (per finding)

- **What** – the finding or problem.
- **Why** – significance and root causes.
- **Impact** – how it reshapes practices, tech, or outcomes (e.g. retention, cost).

## Actionable insights

Propose **one practical application** per finding for the user’s context (e.g. B2B SaaS platform). Quantify benefits where possible. Include one short-term next step and resource estimate.

## Forward projection

- One **tech-debt or execution risk**.
- One **future opportunity**.

## 360° evaluation

Assess impact on: **code**, **UX**, **GTM**, **culture**.

## Format

- Concise report with **3–5** discoveries or problems in bullet points.
- Close with **one reflection question** for continuous improvement.

## Constraints

- Avoid jargon; prioritize radical clarity.
- Verify via web or search when possible.
- If insufficient data for the window, note the gap and ask for clarification.
